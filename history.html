HISTORY


Introduction


Kampung Melayu Subang is a village located in Shah Alam, in Selangor, which is a state in Malaysia.

The village was opened around the 1930s by people from the other side of Java.

Early settlers was named Imam Abdul Hamid and his group of followers.

Imam Abdul Hamid was the first Imam for the mosque in this village and he is highly respected by the local people.

short, it can be said that the early settlers of this village consisted of people of Javanese descent.

This area was originally inhabited by the Asli people.


Historical Events

    The name "Kampung Melayu Subang" originated from a local community story.

    The area is located where two rivers, Sungai Damansara and Sungai Pelumut, meet at Damansara and Sungai Pelumut.

    Native people fishing in the rivers discovered an earring commonly worn by women from both areas.

    The village was subsequently renamed Kampung Subang to reflect this discovery.


Development

  • Economic Shift: Kampung Melayu Subang transitioned from agriculture to a more diverse economy due to development and modernization.

  • Airport Influence: Proximity to Subang International Airport brought job opportunities and led villagers to pursue employment in various sectors.

  • Population Growth: The village experienced an influx of immigrants, prompting the construction of rental houses and contributing to economic growth.
